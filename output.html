Naloxone Chemical reverses the antihypertensive effect of clonidine Chemical . In unanesthetized, spontaneously hypertensive Disease rats the decrease in blood pressure and heart rate produced by intravenous clonidine Chemical , 5 to 20 micrograms/kg, was inhibited or reversed by nalozone Chemical , 0.2 to 2 mg/kg. The hypotensive Disease effect of 100 mg/kg alpha-methyldopa Chemical was also partially reversed by naloxone Chemical . Naloxone Chemical alone did not affect either blood pressure or heart rate. In brain membranes from spontaneously hypertensive Disease rats clonidine Chemical , 10(-8) to 10(-5) M, did not influence stereoselective binding of [3H]-naloxone Chemical (8 nM), and naloxone Chemical , 10(-8) to 10(-4) M, did not influence clonidine Chemical -suppressible binding of [3H]-dihydroergocryptine Chemical (1 nM). These findings indicate that in spontaneously hypertensive Disease rats the effects of central alpha-adrenoceptor stimulation involve activation of opiate receptors. As naloxone Chemical and clonidine Chemical do not appear to interact with the same receptor site, the observed functional antagonism suggests the release of an endogenous opiate by clonidine Chemical or alpha-methyldopa Chemical and the possible role of the opiate in the central control of sympathetic tone.
